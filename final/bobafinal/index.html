T4 #5

T4 was my favorite boba place freshman year. The drinks were the cheap, consistent, and I could get my favorite roasted oolong milk tea. It also had bonus points for being close to Chipotle. However, now the drinks are more expensive for about the same quality-which is okay, but not worth spending $6+. Other places are now higher on this list.

Reasons to Go

  • Good classic drinks
  • Snacks are pretty good
  • Lots of seating

Reasons to Stay Home

  • Raised their prices but the quality and quantity are the same
  • Harder to park

Teapercent

Teapercent is new boba place in Davis, but super popular in Sacramento. The drinks are high quality, but I haven't found one that I absolutely love. This location also has a Mochinut inside which means its easy to get hot dogs and donuts as snacks. Unfortunately, there is not much seating.

Reasons to Go

  • Can also get hotdogs and mochinut donuts
  • Smoothies are pretty good

Reasons to Stay Home

  • Pretty expensive
  • Not much seating
  • Drinks can be hit or miss

UME Tea #3

Ume Tea is #3 on this list. A recent addition to the UC Davis boba scene, but a staple in the Bay Area. Ume is known for it's unique drinks like the Bunny Surprise which comes with a plushie in the bottom of the cup! Not every drink is my favorite, but you won't be able to find these anywhere else.

Reasons to Go

  • Easy parking
  • Unique drinks
  • Seating

Reasons to Stay Home

  • Pretty expensive for the quantity of drink
  • Only a fan of a couple drinks

Teaspoon

Teaspoon is my go to place when I'm at home in San Jose. No matter the location, the drinks and the boba are always good. I've tried almost everything on the menu, but my favorite is the Thai Tea with Classic Cream.

Reasons to Go

  • Good quality boba (pearls)
  • Drinks are consistently good
  • Rewards system

Reasons to Stay Home

  • Hard to find parking
  • No seating
